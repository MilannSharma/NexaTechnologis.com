PILLAR 1: AI PRESENCE & DISCOVERY
The internet is shifting from keyword-based search to AI-generated answers. Google is no longer the only gateway. ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Copilot are now the first stop for millions of queries daily. Companies that do not appear in AI answers are invisible to a growing segment of their market. Nexa Technology solves this through a complete AI presence stack.
1.1  GEO — Generative Engine Optimization
The foundational service. GEO is the practice of optimizing how a brand appears in AI-generated responses across all major language models. Unlike traditional SEO which targets crawlers, GEO targets the reasoning patterns of LLMs — how they weigh authority, how they select sources, and how they generate recommendations.
What We Do:
Audit current AI visibility across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Copilot for 200+ relevant queries
Identify the gap between how AI describes the brand versus reality
Restructure content to match LLM training data preferences — authoritative, concise, well-sourced
Build citation networks — getting the brand cited in sources that LLMs trust and reference
Implement semantic content clustering so LLMs understand the brand's full context
Monthly AI visibility scoring with competitive benchmarking
Deliverables:
AI Visibility Audit Report
GEO Content Strategy
Monthly AI Mention Tracking Dashboard
Competitive AI Recommendation Analysis
1.2  AEO — Answer Engine Optimization
AEO is GEO applied specifically to direct answer slots. When someone asks 'What is the best school ERP in India?' — the AI gives one answer, not ten links. AEO optimizes to win that slot.
What We Do:
Identify every question type where the brand should appear as the direct answer
Write Answer-First content structures — concise authority statements followed by supporting depth
Implement comprehensive schema markup: FAQ, HowTo, Organization, Product, Speakable
Build structured Q&A content libraries that LLMs pull from directly
Optimize for voice-based AI queries — different phrasing, shorter answers, conversational structure
Track answer slot ownership over time
1.3  Entity Optimization
AI systems think in entities, not keywords. An entity is a recognized, distinct thing in the world — a company, a person, a product, a place. If AI systems do not recognize a brand as a legitimate entity, it will not mention or recommend it with confidence.
What We Do:
Wikipedia presence creation or optimization
Wikidata entity setup with complete, accurate structured data
Google Knowledge Panel claiming, verification, and management
Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Bloomberg, and industry directory entity consistency
Founding team entity building — key executives as recognized entities linked to the brand
Product and service entity mapping — every offering as a distinct, searchable entity
Cross-platform entity signal consistency audit and correction
1.4  Knowledge Graph Building

What We Do:
Complete entity relationship mapping for the brand

FUTURE OPPORTUNITIES (2027-2030)
These are emerging markets that do not fully exist yet but will be major categories within 3-4 years. Nexa Technology's technical depth positions it to enter these markets before most competitors recognize them.
AI Memory & Continuity Layer
Current AI systems have no persistent memory between sessions. As AI agents become embedded in business operations, companies will need a managed memory infrastructure — a system that gives AI agents persistent context about the company, its customers, and its history. This is a fundamental infrastructure layer that will be required for serious AI deployments.
AI Workforce Management Platform
As the ratio of AI agents to human employees increases, companies will need entirely new systems to manage their mixed human-AI workforce — task allocation, performance monitoring, cost optimization, and compliance. Traditional HRMS systems cannot handle AI agents. This is a multi-billion-dollar market being created by the AI adoption wave.
Synthetic Data Marketplace
AI model training requires massive datasets that often do not exist for specific domains or are impossible to collect due to privacy regulations. Synthetic data — AI-generated training data that is realistic but not real — solves this. A marketplace and generation service for domain-specific synthetic training data will be a critical infrastructure business.
AI Output Insurance & Verification
As companies rely on AI for decisions with financial and legal consequences, errors become costly. An insurance and verification layer — systems that audit AI outputs for accuracy, flag high-risk decisions, and provide liability coverage for AI errors — will become a required product for enterprise AI deployments.
Personal AI Infrastructure for Enterprises
The next evolution of enterprise software is personal AI — every employee has an AI assistant that knows their role, their work history, their communication style, and their company context. Building and managing this personal AI infrastructure — the underlying systems, data pipelines, and access controls — is a massive market that no company has fully addressed.
AI-Powered Due Diligence Platform
M&A transactions, investment decisions, and partnership evaluations require extensive due diligence — reviewing thousands of documents, contracts, financial records, and communications. An AI platform that automates this process with high accuracy could compress due diligence timelines from months to days. Law firms, PE funds, and corporate development teams are an extremely high-value market.
